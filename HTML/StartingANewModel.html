Starting a New Model

So, you are new to the LIMBS framework, and you’re not sure exactly where to start. Well, then you are in the same situation as our mutual friend, Susan, who is creating her first agent based model of her graduate studies to model how emotional transfer affects stock market booms and busts. She will occasionally be joining us during our exploration into the LIMBS framework, so don’t worry. You’re not alone.

So, once you have your computer started up and you’ve started running an instance of the LIMBS framework, you are ready to start creating a model. Creating a model relies on you following a design process, and although you are ultimately the only person who knows the correct design process for you, we recommend one that works pretty well for the LIMBS framework.

The meeting went quicker than Susan had originally thought... and now she had another assignment to add to the pile.

Susan sat down at her desk and looked at the computer screen sitting in front of her. "Where to start... where to start?" She looked at the blank screen again and sighed. "Well, I guess I should probably turn the computer on first."

Design Process:

  1. Determine the Propositions, Agents and Groups in your model
  2. Arrange the Propositions and Agents into Groups
  3. Create communication links
  4. Customize your agents!
  5. Run your model
  6. Save your model for later

But before we get started, we’ll need to have a working knowledge of the different parts of the system-wide interface. There are three main areas in this workspace: the canvas, the toolkit and the properties field. The canvas is where you can see your model as it currently is. The toolkit bar at the top of the screen has all the entities like propositions, agents and groups, which you can add to the model by selecting them, then dragging and dropping them onto the canvas. Lastly, when you click on an entity on the canvas, the property field fills with all the properties and configurations of that one entity. Here you can edit each item’s name and maybe even some of its characteristics! When you are finished editing, remember to hit “Update!” just to make sure the changes really stick!

Now that you know the basics of the system-wide interface, you are ready to design your very first LIMBS model!
