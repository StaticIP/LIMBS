Glossary of Terms

Actions A proposition that expresses a preference for an action such as “Watch a horror film.”
Agent An individual, autonomous component of an agent based model, which performs basic processing as well as sending and receiving communication.
Agent Based Model A computational model describing a system composed of multiple autonomous and simple subsystems (called agents) through
Altruism A form of emotional transfer where the goal node emotion vectors are communicated as well as non-emotional information]
Artificial Neural Network A computational model where a series of neurons, or nodes, pass a small vector of information to nodes adjacent to them through a series of weighted synapses, or links.
Cold Thought Cognition with no emotional component
Communication RuleA model used to simplify determining who an agent is supposed to communicate with at what time by always communicating with a specific other agent or group when a predetermined set of HOTCO nodes are all highly positive activated.
Contagion A form of emotional transfer where the action node emotion vectors are communicated as well as non-emotional information
Emotion Vector A numeric vector that expresses the emotion associated with an individual node, or an individual agent
Emotional Intensity A component of the emotion vector that expresses the strength or level of arousal associated with an emotion
Emotional Transfer The communication of emotion through messages similar to the communication of non-emotional information such as Evidence, Action or Goals
Emotional Valence A component of the emotion vector that expresses the level of pleasantness associated with an emotion
Empathy A form of emotional transfer where the evidence node emotion vectors are communicated as well as non-emotional information
Evidence A proposition that expresses a proposition of evidence or opinion, such as “Romantic comedies are not thrilling.”
Excitatory Synapse / Excitatory Link A synapse or link in an artificial neural network that increases the activation of the adjacent nodes, often towards a fixed point or maximum
Goal A proposition that expresses a goal to be achieved through actions or acceptance of evidence such as “Feel excited.”
Group A collection of agents and propositions in a LIMBS model. Groups are used to organize communication, and the sharing and co-ordination of propositions across multiple agents.
Hot Thought Cognition with an affect, or emotional, component
HOTCO Acronym for “hot coherence.” A psychologically plausible, artificial neural network model of human cognition reliant on coherence between multiple competing propositions, each holding a truth value and emotional vector
Inhibitory Synapse / Inhibitory Link A synapse or link in an artificial neural network that decreases the activation of the adjacent nodes, often towards a fixed point or minimum
Multi-Brain SystemAn agent based model where agents are all considered psychologically plausible
Node A unit in an artificial neural network, specifically a HOTCO model in this case, which has an activation and an emotion vector
Psychological PlausibilityExplainable through and coherent with the current body of psychological knowledge
Proposition The basic unit of communication and decision making from a system-wide level. Propositions are grouped into Evidence, Actions and Goals.
